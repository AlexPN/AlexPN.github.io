Tools & Materials

For this kind of project it is important to have the right tools first. Of course there are significantly more optimal tools available for this kind of project, but I was constrained by a budget and what I already had. Here is what I used throughout the project:

Tools

  • Mini-lathe (7"x14"), useful throughout the project to make concentric, cylindrical objects/surfaces

  • Drill press (1/3 HP), there is a lot of drilling and tapping for making connections

  • Taps & Dies (of various sizes), this depends on what size bolts you have avilable and if you have either metric or imperial measurements. This can be mostly bypassed if you have access to a welder.

  • CNC milling machine (7"x7"), extremely useful and a timesaver on the more complex parts, but not necessary

  • Hacksaw and/or angle grinder, needed to cut down raw materials to size for use on the lathe or CNC

As well, here is the list of materials that were used. I went with plywood initially for prototyping then switched over to aluminum. Be careful when working with aluminum, it expands and sticks significantly (you can easily break drill bits and taps)!

Materials

  • 3/4" Plywood, used this for prototyping the initial design

  • 1/2" Aluminum plate, was cut into pieces and machined for different parts

  • Planetary geared stepper motors (99.05:1, 200s/r, NEMA17), got these for their incredible torque at low power and relatively low backlash

  • Various nuts, washers, bolts, should be obvious

  • Teensy 3.6, microcontroller for interfacing with the steppers

  • Threaded rod, I used a 3/8" threaded rod to perform alt adjustment for polar alignment
